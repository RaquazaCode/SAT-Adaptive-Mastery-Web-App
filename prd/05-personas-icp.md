# Personas, ICP, and Consumer Journey

## Primary Persona: High-Achieving Student (Target: 1400+)

### Demographics
- Age: 16-18
- Grade: 11th-12th
- Location: Suburban/urban, competitive high school
- Parent income: Upper-middle to upper class
- College goals: Top 50 universities, merit scholarships

### Psychographics
- **Motivation**: Maximize score for competitive admissions and scholarships
- **Pain Points**: 
  - Generic prep courses don't address specific weaknesses
  - Human tutors are expensive ($100-200/hour) and hard to schedule
  - Need precision on routing mastery (Module 1 performance critical)
  - Time-constrained (AP classes, extracurriculars)
- **Goals**: 
  - Score 1400+ (ideally 1500+)
  - Master routing to M2_H module
  - Understand elite strategies (700 vs 800 differentiation)
- **Language**: Uses terms like "routing," "module performance," "skill gaps"

### Why Personalized Tutor Over Alternatives
- Generic prep: "Doesn't know my specific error patterns"
- Human tutor: "Too expensive, can't practice at 11pm"
- Self-study: "Don't know what to focus on"

### Emotional Triggers
- Fear: Missing target score = missing dream school
- Desire: Efficiency and precision
- Need: Validation that they're improving

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Core Characteristics
- **Stage**: Preparing for Digital SAT (not ACT-focused)
- **Timeline**: 2-6 months before test date
- **Willingness to pay**: $20-50/month (vs. $500-2000 for human tutor)
- **Tech comfort**: High (comfortable with digital tools)
- **Learning style**: Prefers structured, data-driven approach

### Decision Criteria
1. **Personalization**: "Does it adapt to my specific needs?"
2. **Availability**: "Can I practice when I have time?"
3. **Feedback quality**: "Do I understand why I got things wrong?"
4. **Progress tracking**: "Can I see I'm improving?"
5. **Cost**: "Is it affordable compared to alternatives?"

### What Makes Them Choose Personalized Tutor
- **Bloom Two-Sigma Effect**: Understands 1-on-1 tutoring is proven most effective
- **Accessibility**: Available 24/7 vs. scheduled sessions
- **Affordability**: Fraction of human tutor cost
- **Learning**: Gets smarter about them over time
- **Support**: Provides encouragement, not just content

## Consumer Journey Map

### Stage 1: Awareness
**Trigger**: "I need SAT help but tutors are expensive"

**Touchpoints**:
- Google search: "affordable SAT prep"
- Social media: Friend shares score improvement
- School counselor recommendation

**Messaging**:
- "Get the power of 1-on-1 tutoring at a fraction of the cost"
- "Your personal SAT tutor available 24/7"
- "Adapts to your schedule, learns your patterns"

**Emotional State**: Overwhelmed, uncertain

---

### Stage 2: Consideration
**Trigger**: "How does this compare to Khan Academy/Princeton Review?"

**Touchpoints**:
- Landing page visit
- Feature comparison
- FAQ reading
- Free diagnostic test

**Messaging**:
- Comparison: Generic prep vs. Human tutors vs. Your Personal Tutor
- "Unlike generic courses, your tutor learns your specific error patterns"
- "Unlike human tutors, available whenever you need, never judges"

**Emotional State**: Comparing options, skeptical

---

### Stage 3: Decision
**Trigger**: "This gives me a personal tutor that learns my patterns"

**Touchpoints**:
- Free diagnostic test completion
- Seeing personalized feedback
- Understanding the tutor relationship

**Messaging**:
- "Start your free diagnostic - your tutor will assess you"
- "See how your tutor identifies your weaknesses"
- "Watch your tutor get smarter about you"

**Emotional State**: Hopeful, ready to try

---

### Stage 4: Onboarding
**Trigger**: First diagnostic, seeing personalized feedback

**Touchpoints**:

**Messaging**:

---

### Stage 6: Advocacy

## Key Messaging by Stage

### Awareness → Consideration
- Focus: "Personal tutor" not "adaptive system"
- Emphasize: Availability, affordability, personalization
- Avoid: Technical jargon, "AI" word

### Consideration → Decision
- Focus: Comparison to alternatives
- Emphasize: What makes personalized tutor unique
- Proof: Free diagnostic shows value

### Decision → Onboarding
- Focus: "Your tutor knows you"
- Emphasize: Personalization, specific feedback
- Action: Start first practice session

### Onboarding → Engagement
- Focus: Progress and improvement
- Emphasize: Tutor learning about you
- Support: Encouragement, not judgment

### Engagement → Advocacy
- Focus: Results and transformation
- Emphasize: Tutor's role in success
- Action: Share, refer, continue
